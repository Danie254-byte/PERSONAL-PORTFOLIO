Where I Am Now

I'm currently in my second year of studying Software Engineering at university. It's been a journey ‚Äî sometimes exciting, sometimes overwhelming, but always pushing me to learn something new.

Choosing software engineering wasn't a random decision. After years of being curious about how technology works, it felt like the natural path to actually understand and create it myself. Plus, the tech industry in Kenya is growing, and I want to be part of that growth.

What I'm Learning

University has introduced me to so many things I didn't know existed. It's not just about writing code, though there's plenty of that. It's about understanding systems, thinking logically, and solving problems in structured ways.

Core Subjects I've Covered:

  • Programming Fundamentals ‚Äî Started with the basics, learned how to think like a programmer
  • Web Development ‚Äî HTML, CSS, JavaScript ‚Äî building things for the internet
  • Database Management ‚Äî How to store and organize data properly
  • Mathematics ‚Äî Discrete math, calculus ‚Äî the foundation for everything
  • Computer Networks ‚Äî How computers talk to each other
  • Software Engineering Principles ‚Äî Best practices for building reliable software

Learning Through Projects

The best learning happens when you actually build something. Class projects have taught me more than lectures sometimes. When you're trying to make something work and it keeps breaking, you learn fast.

Group projects have been especially valuable. Working with classmates who think differently from you, dealing with deadlines, figuring out how to divide work fairly these are skills that go beyond coding.

Skills I've Developed

Problem Solving

Breaking down big problems into smaller, manageable pieces. It's not always easy, but it works.

Teamwork

Learning to communicate clearly, share ideas, and sometimes disagree respectfully with teammates.

Time Management

Balancing coursework, DJ gigs, and personal life. Still working on this one, honestly.

Self-Learning

University teaches you the basics, but you have to dig deeper on your own. YouTube tutorials are my friend.

The Challenges

I won't pretend it's been easy. Some courses are genuinely difficult. There have been nights where I've stared at code for hours without understanding why it won't work. There have been exams I wasn't prepared for and projects that didn't turn out as planned.

But I think that's part of the process. Every time I struggle through something difficult, I come out a bit stronger and more capable. The frustration is temporary; the skills stick around.

Balancing Studies and DJing

One thing I've had to figure out is how to balance my academic work with my DJ career. It's not always easy. Sometimes I have a gig on a Friday night and an assignment due Monday morning. But I've learned to plan ahead and prioritize.

Interestingly, I think both activities complement each other. The creativity I use in music helps me think differently about coding problems. And the logical thinking from programming helps me organize my music and plan sets better.

What's Next

I still have a couple of years left in my degree. There's so much more to learn about mobile app development, cloud computing, maybe some artificial intelligence. The field is constantly evolving, and that's both exciting and a little intimidating.

But I'm committed to seeing this through and coming out as a capable software engineer who can actually build useful things.

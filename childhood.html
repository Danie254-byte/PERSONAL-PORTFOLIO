Where It All Started

My Childhood

Growing up in Kenya with a love for music and computers

Growing Up in Kenya

I grew up in Kenya a town in Trans-nzoia that is Kitale, and honestly, my childhood was pretty normal. Nothing too dramatic or special, just a kid trying to figure out what he liked and what he wanted to do with his life. But looking back, there were definitely some signs of who I was going to become.

The Curious Kid

I was always that kid who wanted to know how things worked. If something was broken at home, I'd be the first one trying to open it up and see what was inside. Radios, old phones, even my dad's watch once (of course he wasn't too happy about that one). I just couldn't help it and I needed to understand what made things work.

Computers fascinated me the most. The first time I used one, I remember being completely amazed that you could type something and it would just... appear on the screen. It sounds silly now, but back then it felt like magic to me.

Music Was Always There

Music was a big part of our household. My parents played all kinds of music starting from gospel, oldies, some local Kenyan hits. I remember listening to the radio and trying to figure out why some songs just made you want to move, while others didn't have the same effect.

I wasn't thinking about becoming a DJ back then,that came much later. But I was definitely paying attention to beats and rhythms more than most kids my age. My friends would be playing outside and nicknaming me 'DJ', and I'd be inside listening to the same song on repeat, trying to understand its structure.

School Days

School was okay. I wasn't the top student, but I wasn't struggling either. I did well in subjects I cared about and just got by in the ones I didn't. Looking back, I wish I had paid more attention in some classes, but that's how it goes when you're young ‚Äî you don't always see the bigger picture.

The subjects I liked most were anything that let me be creative or solve problems. Physics class was fun. Math made sense to me most of the time. Computer studies, once we had that, became my favorite period of the week.

First Experiences with Technology

My first real experience with technology beyond just using it was in secondary school. We had a computer lab, and I spent as much time there as I could. I learned the basics like typing, using Microsoft Office, some simple stuff with the internet.

But what really got me excited was when I discovered that you could actually make things with computers. Not just use programs other people made, but create your own. That realization changed everything for me.

The Seeds Were Planted

By the time I finished secondary school, I knew two things: I wanted to do something with computers, and I wanted music to be part of my life somehow. I didn't know exactly how those two things would come together, but I knew they were important to me.

That's how my childhood set the stage for where I am now studying software engineering while pursuing my passion for DJing on the side. It's a combination that makes sense to me, even if it seems unusual to some people.
